How long a debt can be collected by the CRA

The CRA can only collect a debt during the legislated collections limitation period. This period starts and ends at different times depending on the type of debt you owe, and can be restarted or extended when certain actions happen.

How long we can collect different types of debts

We can usually collect on a debt 6 or 10 years from the date the collections limitation period starts. The start date and length of the limitation period depends on the type of debt you owe.

When the period is restarted or extended

A limitation period can be restarted or extended when certain actions happen. This gives us a longer time to collect your debt.

Actions that will restart a limitation period

Here are a few examples of actions that will restart the collections limitation period:

Actions you take

  • Making a voluntary payment
  • Writing a letter to the CRA to propose a payment arrangement
  • Providing a written acknowledgment of debt
  • Offering to provide security instead of paying the amount owed
  • Making a written request for a reassessment
  • Filing a notice of objection
  • Filing an appeal
  • Asking the CRA if you can make pre-authorized debt payments

Actions we take

  • Issuing a garnishment or set-off to collect an outstanding tax debt when you don’t make voluntary payments
  • Applying a tax refund or another credit (such as GST/HST credit refund) to debt
  • Issuing a notice of assessment or reassessment against a third party for amounts you owe
  • Certifying your tax debt in the Federal Court of Canada
  • Initiating seizure and sale of assets to collect your outstanding debt

Actions that will extend a limitation period

A collections limitation period can be extended when certain events happen. This means the collections limitation period will be put on pause when we are notified of the event starting. When the event is over, the collections limitation period restarts where it left off. It may take more than the 6 or 10 years to reach the end of the collections limitation period.

For example, if there was an event that extended a 6-year collections limitation period by 2 years, it would actually take 8 years to reach the collections limitation period

Here are some examples of actions that will extend the collections limitation period:

Actions you take

  • Filing for bankruptcy or making a proposal under the Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act, Farm Debt Mediation Act, or Companies’ Creditors Arrangement Act
  • Becoming a non-resident of Canada after the CRA issues a notice of assessment or reassessment
  • Filing a notice of objectionFootnote 1
  • Filing an appealFootnote 1
  • Being ordered by the court to make payments as part of your sentence (EI only)

Actions we take

  • Accepting security instead of payment of your debt
  • Postponing collection action without accepting security for your objected or appealed GST/HST debtFootnote 1

What happens when the limitation period ends

For most programs, we cannot take any other action to collect your debt when the collections limitation period ends. However, your debt still remains and you can make voluntary payments. Voluntary payments you make after the CLP ends will not restart it. You should not make any payments for an Employment Insurance debt after the CLP is expired.

This doesn’t apply to Canada Student Loans or Canada Apprentice Loans. When you make a voluntary payment for these programs, it will restart the CLP even if it’s expired.

Footnotes

Footnote 1

This will extend the collections limitation period only for tax debts subject to collection restrictions.
