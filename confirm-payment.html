Payments to the CRA

Confirm an amount to pay or if your payment was received

You can confirm that you have a balance due to the CRA. If you make a payment to the CRA, it may not be applied to your account right away. It will be applied when your payment is processed by the CRA.

Check your balance online

Before checking to see if the CRA has received a payment you sent, allow:

  • 3 business days for online payments
  • 10 business days (plus mailing time) for payments by cheque or money order

Confirm amounts you owe the CRA and payments you have made by checking your online CRA account or calling us.

  • Online

    Check your balance in:

    • My Account
    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client
  • By phone

    Call us to confirm an amount you need to pay, or resolve an issue with a payment you sent

Online
  1. Determine if you can set it up online

    You can schedule a series of payments in My Account, My Business Account or Represent a Client for the following debts:

    My Account

    • Personal income tax

    My Business Account

    • Corporate income tax
    • Payroll deductions
    • GST/HST
    • Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS)
    • Excise duty, excise tax or excise tax on insurance premiums
    • Air travellers security charge (ATSC)
    • Fuel charge
    • Partnerships

    Other payments not accepted

    • If you are trying to pay another type of debt over time, use the by phone option.
  2. Sign in to your CRA online account

    Sign in to your account and select "Proceed to pay" then "Schedule a series of payments".

    CRA sign in

    Pay on behalf of someone else

    To schedule payments on behalf of a family member or a business you represent, sign in to Represent a Client.

  3. Confirm a PAD agreement

    After you determine a payment schedule that works best for you with the calculator available in your CRA account, confirm your PAD agreement.

By phone

  1. Before you call

    When you call, you’ll need to give:

    • Social Insurance Number (or business number)
    • Full name and date of birth (or business name)
    • Your complete address (or complete business address)
    • Your personal (or business) account open or an assessed return, notice of assessment, reassessment, or other tax document

  2. Call the CRA to set up your payment arrangement

    Call us about other debt

    To call us or to request a call for other types of debt, such as for a benefit overpayment, student loan, CERB or a business debt, refer to: Call us to discuss your debt

  3. Start your payment arrangement

    To start your payment arrangement, you need to make your first payment towards your debt.

    You must continue to make payments on the agreed upon dates in your payment arrangement and file all future tax returns on time. Your payment arrangement may be reviewed periodically.

    For options, go to make a payment.

Check your account balance in CRA My Account

Individuals can log in to CRA My Account to view payment details under "Accounts and payments".

Check your account balance in My Business Account

Businesses can log in to My Business Account to view payment details under "View and pay account balance".

Resolve an issue with a payment

If you had a debt on your account, we may have automatically used your refund, benefit or credit to pay down the debt: How we automatically apply refunds and credits to your debt

If your individual tax or benefit payment was not applied to an account as expected, you or your representative can contact us:

  • Canadian residents: 1-800-959-8281 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-8281
  • Non-residents
    • Canada or U.S.: 1-855-284-5946 (opens up phone application)1-855-284-5946
    • Outside Canada or U.S.: 1-613-940-8499 (opens up phone application)1-613-940-8499

If your business payment was not applied to an account as expected, you or your representative can:

  • Move business payments or balances between accounts
  • Call 1-800-959-5525 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-5525

When calling the CRA to resolve payment issues, you or your representative will need to provide additional information, such as details from one of your assessed tax returns, to verify your identity and ensure confidentiality.
