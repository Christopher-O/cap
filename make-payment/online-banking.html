Pay online with your bank or credit union: Sending a payment to the CRA

You can make a payment or schedule future payment(s) to the CRA through the online banking account or mobile banking app of most Canadian banks or credit unions.

  • How to pay the amount you owe

What you can and can't pay

We accept the following payments online through your bank or credit union:

  • most individual and business payments
All payments accepted online with a bank or credit union
  • the other one
  • and another

We do not accept the following individuals payments online with a bank or credit union:

  • Climate Action Incentive (CAI) repayment/overpayment
  • Alberta credits repayment/overpayment
  • TFSA amoung owing

We do not accept the following business payments online through your bank or credit union except the following:

  • Corporate income tax instalment payments
  • Information Return (RZ), T5, RRSP, TFSA, etc
  • Information returns penalty
  • Canada Emergency Rent Subsidy (CERS) repayment
  • Garnishment on behalf of an employee

How to pay the amount you owe

Make a payment for yourself, on behalf of another, or for a business through your bank or credit union's online banking account or mobile banking app. Most financial institutions let you set up a payment to be made on a future date.

    2. Sign in to your financial institution's online banking service for individuals.
    3. Ensure you select the correct option under "Add a payee" to avoid misallocated payments:
      Pay your tax owing before assessment

      If you submitted your tax return and ........................

      Select an option such as "CRA (revenue) – 2022 tax return"

      Pay an assessed tax owing

      Select an option such as "CRA (revenue) – tax amount owing"

      Pay a tax instalment

      Select an option such as "CRA (revenue) – tax instalment"

      Repay COVID-19 benefits

      Select an option such as "CRA (revenue) – Canada emergency benefit repayment"

      Repay other benefits and credits

      Select an option such as "CRA (revenue) – tax amount owing"

      Pay a student loan or apprenticeship grant

      Select an option such as "Employment and Social Development Canada" The ESDC site appears to say to select "National Student Loans Center" instead of ESDC? Why are these different?

    4. Enter your 9-digit social insurance number as your account number.

        Many of these financial institutions also provide business customers with non-resident payment options.

    2. Sign in to your financial institution's tax filing service or online banking service for businesses. Check with your financial institution if you can't find business payment options.
    3. Select the tax payment type you are trying to pay:
      Pay corporation tax

      Select an option such as "Federal – Corporation Tax Payments – TXINS"

      Remit GST/HST

      Select an option such as "Federal – GST/HST Payment – GST-P (GST-P)"

      Remit payroll

      Depending on how you remit payroll, you need to select one of the following options:

      • "Federal Payroll Deductions – Regular/Quarterly – EMPTX – (PD7A)"
      • "Federal Payroll Deductions – Threshold 1 – EMPTX – (PD7A)"
      • "Federal Payroll Deductions – Threshold 2 – EMPTX – (PD7A)"
      Repay COVID subsidy overpayments

      Select an option such as "Federal - Canada emergency wage subsidy repayment"

      What about other COVID subsidies? CERS, THRP, HHBRP?
      Pay a customs tax debt

      You may only pay a customs tax debt in collections online with a bank or credit union with an importer's business number (RM account).

      To find out what payee to select for your financial institution:

      • Go to Canada Border Services Agency
      • Scroll down and click on “Expand All”
      • Under “Option 1 - Online Banking” note the payee names

      From your financial institution’s website:

      • Select the appropriate payee name for your financial institution
      • Add your 9 digit Business Number with your RM identification (for example 123456789 RM0005)
      What are other missing options we may need to include?

    4. Follow the instructions which would include entering your 15-digit business number as your CRA account number.

How long it takes to be received and processed

  • Received by CRA: same day it was paid (stamped by the bank employee)
  • Shows up in your CRA account: up to 2 business days after the payment is received. Confirm if a payment was received
