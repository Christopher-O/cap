Pay without a Canadian bank account: Sending a payment to the CRA

Non-residents who do not have a Canadian bank account can make their payments to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) by wire transfer or with an internationally issued credit card through a third-party service provider who charge a fee for their services.

Note: Payments for non-resident GST/HST security deposits cannot be made by wire transfer at this time.

What you need to know

All wire transfers must be in Canadian dollars.

Your financial institution may charge you a fee to make a wire transfer payment. Make sure that your financial institution does not deduct the wire transfer fee from the total payment amount due as this will result in an underpayment.

New:

Wire transfers are now processed in Canada by Fédération des Caisse Desjardins du Québec. They do not charge any fee for forwarding your wire transfer payment to the Canada Revenue Agency.

Your details

Wire space is limited, make sure the following details are provided for your payment to be credited to the correct account:

For individuals your:

  • non-resident account number or social insurance number
  • full legal name
  • telephone number
  • tax year/year end

Example: 599999976, John Smith, 613-000-0000

For businesses include the:

  • non-resident account number or business number
  • business name
  • period end date
  • telephone number

Example: 777777798RP0001, Smith Construction, 2019/03/15, 613-000-0000

Send fax

What you can and can't pay

We accept the following payments by wire transfer:

Individual:

  • Individual income tax amount owing
  • Individual income tax instalment payments
  • Canada dental benefit repayment/overpayment
  • One time top-up to Canada housing benefit repayment/overpayment

Business:

  • Nova Scotia Workers' Compensation Board
  • COVID overpayments (CEWS, etc.)
  • Corporation income tax (T2)
  • GST/HST
  • Source deductions (payroll)
  • Information returns penalty
  • Excise duties
  • Excise tax (various things)
  • Fuel charge
  • Luxury tax
  • Air travellers Security Charge
  • Excise tax on insurance premiums

We do not accept the following individuals payments online through wire transfer:

  • Individual income tax instalment payments- NO CALC. OPTION
  • COVID overpayment (CERB, etc.)
  • Canada Child Benefit(CCB) repayment/overpayment
  • GST/HST credit repayment/overpayment
  • Universal child benefits
  • Climate Action Incentive (CAI) repayment/overpayment
  • Alberta credits repayment/overpayment
  • Ontario credits repayment/overpayment
  • Tax free savings account- AMOUNT OWING
  • Part XIII withholding tax
  • Security deposit (Part IX) incl. GST/HST

We do not accept the following business payments online through wire transfer:

  • Underused housing tax
  • Workplace Safety and Insurance Board (Ontario)
  • Various provincial/territorial benefit/credit overpayments
  • Corporate income tax instalment payments
  • Information Return (RZ), T5, RRSP, TFSA, etc.
  • Rent subsidies repayment
  • Softwood lumber products export charge
  • Garnishment on behalf of an employee

Avoid late fees

You are responsible for making sure the CRA receives your payment by the payment due date. If you are using a third-party service provider, you must clearly understand the terms and conditions of the services you are using.

Please ensure that you set up your payment well in advance of your payment’s due date as payment delivery is not immediate, and is determined by the third party service provider that you use.

A note about endorsements

The CRA does not endorse websites, products, services, or publications. They are provided only for convenience.
