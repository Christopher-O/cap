Payment options: out of

  • Online banking

    Some financial institutions allow you to add the CRA as a payee to make a single or multiple scheduled payments.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawal from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay using your bank or credit union website or app

    • Considered as paid: The same or next business day (varies based on your bank or credit union)
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 2 business days after the payment is received

  • Counter (teller) service at bank or credit union

    In Canada

    You can make a payment to the CRA at any financial institution in Canada.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawal from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay at the counter (teller) of a bank or credit union

    • Considered as paid: The same day it was paid (stamped by employee)
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

    Outside of Canada

    Non-resident financial institutions may be able to wire transfer a payment to the CRA.

    How to pay at a foreign bank or credit union

    • Considered as paid: The same or next business day
    • Updated in your CRA account: Usually 3 business days after the payment is received

  • ATM of a bank or credit union

    Before you go to the ATM you may need to set the CRA up as a payee.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawal from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay at your financial institution's ATM

    • Considered as paid: Within 1 to 2 business days
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 3 business days after the payment is received

  • CRA secure online services

    One-time payments that are not future dated

    Use your debit card to pay using CRA's My Payment service.

    How to pay using My Payment

    • Considered as paid: Usually the same or next business day (varies based on your bank or credit union)
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 3 business days after the payment is received

    Multiple or future-dated payments

    Scheduled payments must be set up at least 5 business days before the first payment is made.

    Set up pre-authorized debit (PAD) payments, scheduled direct withdrawals from your bank account, in CRA My Account or My Business Account.

    How to pay using My Account or My Business Account

    • Considered as paid: The same day as the scheduled withdrawal, as long as the payment is scheduled at least 5 business days in advance
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 2 business days after the payment is received

  • Third-party service providers

    Most service providers will accept a debit card, credit card, or online transfer (e.g. PayPal). The type of payments accepted varies based on the service provider.

    Service fees will be charged by the provider.

    How to pay using a third-party service provider

    • Considered as paid: Varies based on the service provider
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

  • Mail delivery

    You can send a cheque or a series of postdated cheques with or without a paper return through the mail.

    How to pay by mail

    • Considered as paid: When delivered to the CRA, which depends on the mail delivery time
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 10 business days after the payment is received

  • Canada Post retail location

    Before you pay at Canada Post you must have a customized QR code to apply the payment correctly.

    Service fees will be charged.

    You can pay with a debit card or cash.

    How to pay at a Canada Post retail location

    • Considered as paid: The same day it was paid (stamped by employee)
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 2 business days after the payment is received

CRA does not accept certain payments

This includes but is not limited to:

  • Cryptocurrency

    We do not accept any form of cryptocurrency (e.g. Bitcoin) as payment

  • Gift cards

    We do not accept any gift cards in any form of payment

  • Travellers cheques

    We do not accept any travellers cheques

  • Cash by mail

    Cash can only be used at any Canada post retail location
