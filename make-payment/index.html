Make a payment: Payments to the CRA

The CRA accepts many payment methods. Find out which way to pay works best for you.

Options for your situation

Some payments can only be made using certain methods. To see what options are available for the specific payment you are making, Answer a few questions about your payment.

Filters

Payment options: out of

  • Online banking

    Some financial institutions allow you to add the CRA as a payee to make a single or multiple scheduled payments.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawal from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay: Bank or credit union website or app

    • Considered as paid: Usually 1 to 2 business days from when you send the payment
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 5 business days after the payment is received

  • Counter (teller) service at bank or credit union

    In Canada

    You can make a payment to the CRA at any financial institution in Canada.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawal from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay: Bank or credit union at the counter (teller)

    • Considered as paid: Usually the same day it was paid (stamped by employee)
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

    Outside of Canada

    Non-resident financial institutions may be able to wire transfer a payment to the CRA.

    Payments can be sent using a wire transfer.

    How to pay: Foreign bank or credit union

    • Considered as paid: Usually the same or next business day
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

  • ATM with bank or credit union

    Before you go to the ATM you may need to have the CRA set up as a payee.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawal from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay: ATM of your financial institution

    • Considered as paid: Usually within 1 to 2 business days
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 2 business days after the payment is received

  • CRA secure online services

    Single payment

    How to pay: by debit card through CRA's My Payment service

    • Considered as paid: Usually the same or next business day
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

    Multiple scheduled payments

    The schedule must be set up no less than 5 business days before the first payment is made through a direct withdrawal from your bank account.

    How to pay: Scheduling a pre-authorized debit (PAD) in My Account, My Business Account, or Represent a Client

    • Considered as paid: Usually the same day as the scheduled withdrawal (if the payment is scheduled at least 5 business days in advance)
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 1 business day after the payment is received

  • Third party service providers

    Not every service provider will process every type of payment.

    Most service providers will accept a debit card, credit card, or online e-transfer (e.g. PayPal). Types of payments that are accepted will vary.

    Service fees will be charged by the provider.

    How to pay: Third-party service provider

    • Considered as paid: Varies based on the service provider
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

  • Mail delivery

    You can send a payment or a series of postdated cheques with or without a paper return through the mail.

    How to pay: By mail

    • Considered as paid: Usually when delivered to CRA, which depends on the mail delivery time
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

  • Canada Post retail location

    Before you pay at Canada Post you must have a customized QR code to apply the payment correctly.

    Service fees will be charged.

    Accepted payments are a debit card or cash.

    How to pay: Visit a Canada Post retail location

    • Considered as paid: Usually the same day it was paid (stamped by employee)
    • Updated in your CRA account: Up to X business days after the payment is received

CRA does not accept certain payments

This includes but is not limited to:

  • Cryptocurrency

    We do not accept any form of cryptocurrency (e.g. Bitcoin) as payment

  • Gift cards

    We do not accept any gift cards in any form of payment

  • Travellers cheques

    We do not accept any travellers cheques

  • Cash by mail

    Cash can only be used at any Canada post retail location
