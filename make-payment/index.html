Payment types: Sending a payment to the CRA

In order to make a payment, you will need to know the options in which you can pay, and any special rules that may apply to the payment type and the way you pay it.

Answer a few questions to find what payment methods you can make to the CRA for your payment type.

Once you make a selection, scroll down for further options or answers.

Here is a list of other areas you may have received a payment from:

  • For ESDC:
    • Online banking

      CRA's "My Payment" is not available to pay an ESDC debt

      Payments can be made online through your financial institution.

      1. Select "Employment and Social Development Canada" as the payee
      2. Enter your Social Insurance Number (SIN) as the account number
      3. If your financial institution requires 11 characters, add the letters "YY" or the numbers "00" at the end on your entry
    • By mail

      If you are unable to make your payments online, you may pay by sending a cheque or money order.

      1. Make your cheque or money order payable to "Receiver General for Canada"
      2. Write your client ID number on the front of your cheque or money order
      3. Include your remittance slip with your payment – do not staple
      4. Mail your payment to the payment office indicated on the back of your statement of account

      Do not send cash by mail.

    • For non-residents

      Foreign currency payments can by made by international wire transfer. Electronic funds transfer must be made in Canadian or US currency.

      Foreign cheques will not be accepted.

      International wire transfers:

      Complete the fields as indicated below:

      • Charges: "OUR "
      • Description (mandatory):
        • Client name: last name, first name
        • Client identification number from remittance slip, invoice and/or statement of account
        • Other information to indicate the intent of the payment (Program: CPP, EI, OAS, etc.)

  • For CBSA:
    • Online (only with an importer's business number "RM account")

      CRA's "My Payment" is not available to pay a CBSA debt

      To find out what payee to select for your financial institution:

      1. Go to Canada Border Services Agency
      2. Scroll down and click on "Expand All"
      3. Under "Option 1 - Online Banking" note the payee names

      From your financial institution’s website:

      1. Select the appropriate payee name for your financial institution
      2. Add your 9 digit Business Number with your RM identification (for example 123456789 RM0005)

      You can pay by debit or credit card for amounts less than $5,000.

      The CBSA does not accept wire transfers as forms of payment.

    • By mail
      1. Make the cheque or money order payable to the "Receiver General for Canada"
      2. Write your business account number or your Importer number on the front of your cheque or money order
      3. To direct a payment, write the invoice number on the cheque or include a list of invoices with your payment – do not staple any documents to your cheque
      4. Mail your payment to:
        • Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) Mailroom
          Attention: Accounts Receivable Management Unit
          333 North River Road, Place Vanier, Tower A
          Ground Floor, Room 1018
          Ottawa, Ontario K1A 0L8
        • Telephone: 343-291-5265 (for courier reference)

      The CBSA does not accept cash or travellers cheques

      Pay at your financial institution
