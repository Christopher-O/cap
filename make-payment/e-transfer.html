How to pay the amount you owe

Make a payment for yourself, on behalf of another, or for a business through your bank or credit union's online banking account or mobile banking app. Most financial institutions let you set up a payment to be made on a future date.

  • Individuals

    Make a payment for yourself or on behalf of another taxpayer for personal income taxes or benefit repayments.

  • Businesses

    You must call both the Benefits and Individuals call centres to check for different types of uncashed cheques you might have, and get the forms mailed to you

    • Personal income tax refunds
    • Tax credit and benefit payments for individuals
    • COVID-19 benefit and subsidy payments
    • Corporate refunds or tax credits
    • Payments made to a trust
Online
  1. Sign in to your financial institution's online banking service for individuals.
  2. Under "Add a payee" look for an option such as:
    • CRA (revenue) – 2022 tax return
      • Use this option to make a payment for your 2022 tax return.
    • CRA (revenue) – tax amount owing
      • Use this option to make a payment for any amount owing, as shown on your notice of assessment / reassessment, on your statement of account, remittance voucher, or in letters issued by the Canada Revenue Agency.
    • CRA (revenue) – tax instalment
      • Use this option to make payments toward your 2023 tax return.
    • CRA (revenue) – Canada emergency benefit repayment
      • Use this option to repay COVID-19 benefits: Canada emergency response benefit (CERB) with the CRA, Canada emergency student benefit (CESB), Canada recovery benefit (CRB), Canada recovery caregiving benefit (CRCB), Canada recovery sickness benefit (CRSB), or Canada worker lockdown benefit (CWLB).
  3. Enter your 9-digit social insurance number as your CRA account number.
    Make sure the number is accurate so that the CRA can apply your payment correctly.
